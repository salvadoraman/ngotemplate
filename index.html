Donations

NGOs often rely on individual donations from supporters, either through one-time contributions or regular donations. Donations can come from a variety of sources, including individuals, foundations, and corporations.

Grants

NGOs may receive grants from governments, foundations, or other organizations to support their programs and operations. Grants may be awarded based on specific criteria or objectives, and may be tied to specific program areas or geographic regions.

(CSR) programs

NGOs may partner with corporations that have CSR programs to support their programs and initiatives. This can include financial support, in-kind donations, or volunteer efforts by employees.

Fundraising Events

NGOs may organize fundraising events, such as charity walks, galas, or auctions, to raise funds for their programs and operations.

Government Funding

Government funding: NGOs may receive funding from government sources, either at the local, national, or international level, to support their programs and initiatives.

Social Imapact Investigating

NGOs may receive investments from individuals or organizations that seek to generate a social or environmental impact, in addition to financial returns.

Earned Income

Earned income: NGOs may generate revenue by selling products or services related to their mission, such as handicrafts, training programs, or consulting services.
